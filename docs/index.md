# Get involved with Doenet

There are many ways to get involved with Doenet. Just by writing activities on [Doenet](https://beta.doenet.org) and sharing them publicly, you are contributing to the content that we can all use.

If you'd like more specific ways to contribute, you are welcome to get involved with these groups.

## Documentation Group

The documentation group writes and updates our [documentation](https://docs.doenet.org) for people getting started with Doenet.
The documentation is a work in progress, and we welcome any contributions, small or large, toward improving it!

To get involved, check out the [documentation discussions](https://community.doenet.org/tag/documentation) or email [docs@doenet.org](mailto:docs@doenet.org).

Documentation group members: Virginia Mae, Jon Rogness

## Curriculum Group

The curriculum group helps shepherd the development of the content on [Doenet](https://beta.doenet.org).
They help organize groups of authors working on different topics, curate content for
[library section](https://beta.doenet.org/explore), and develop our content classification system.

To get involved, check out the [curriculum discussions](https://community.doenet.org/tag/curriculum) or email [curriculum@doenet.org](mailto:curriculum@doenet.org).

Curriculum group members: Kris Hollingsworth, Anurag Katyal, Oscar Levin, Virginia Mae, Jon Rogness, Mike Weimerskirch

## Training Group

The training group helps people get started with using Doenet, with a particular focus in helping people write content.
The group responds to author questions, hosts online office hours, and organizes training workshops.

To get involved, check out the [training discussions](https://community.doenet.org/tag/training) or email [training@doenet.org](mailto:training@doenet.org).

Training group members: Anurag Katyal, Melissa Lynn, Virginia Mae, Duane Nykamp, Ozlem Ugurlu, Mike Weimerskirch

## Communications Group

Interested in telling others about Doenet? Join our communications group, and help us write blog entries, social media posts, and/or a newsletter.

To get involved, check out the [communications discussions](https://community.doenet.org/tag/communications) or email [communications@doenet.org](mailto:communications@doenet.org).

Communication group members: Melissa Lynn, Charles Nykamp, Jon Rogness

## Software Group

The software group developes the software for the Doenet website and the language used to write Doenet activities, called DoenetML (Doenet Markup Language).
You can help the software group by creating bug reports (called Issues) on our [GitHub site](https://github.com/Doenet/DoenetTools/issues) or finding an issue that you can help fix.

To get involved, check out our [Github](https://github.com/orgs/Doenet/discussions) or email [dev@doenet.org](mailto:dev@doenet.org).

Software group members: Melissa Lynn, Charles Nykamp, Duane Nykamp, Jason Siefken

## Accessibility Group

Test accessibility group works to make sure that Doenet is accessible to all. They test the accessibility of the Doenet website and activities, and they work to improve the way users using assistive technology can interact with the content.

To get involved, check out the [accessibility discussions](https://community.doenet.org/tag/accessibility) or email [accessibility@doenet.org](mailto:accessibility@doenet.org).

Accessibility group members: Virginia Mae, Duane Nykamp
